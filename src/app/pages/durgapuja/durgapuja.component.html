NVBA Sarbojanin Durga Utsav

A BIG hello to all of you from the NVBA Executive Committee! Hope you all are doing fine and counting days for Durga Utsav 2022.

We are extremely thrilled to announce our international artists who will be illuminating the stage this Durga Utsav. We bring to you some of the biggest and most happening artists among today’s Bengali music lovers. To enthrall you all, we have an assortment of artists that range from Bangla Band music to Classical/Semi-Classical melodies to Modern Bengali and Bollywood music.

Chandrabindoo: The band that rules every Bengali’s heart and soul will steal your heart again on Friday, September 30.

Chandrabindoo (Bengali: চন্দ্রবিন্দু Chôndrobindu) (often spelled Chandrabindu), based in Kolkata, is a bangla band. Their name refers both to the last character in the Bengali alphabet and to a dialogue (very famous among Bengalis) from the nonsense work HoJoBoRoLo by Sukumar Ray. The band is known for Chandril's satirical, colloquial lyrics, full of references to current affairs and cultural phenomena. They also sing other types of song, written by Anindyo. They are well known in Kolkata, Delhi and Mumbai and have recently started touring internationally including a performance in aid of the Kolkata based Child In Need Institute (CINI) in London.

বেড়াল বলল, “বেড়ালও বলতে পার, রুমালও বলতে পার, চন্দ্রবিন্দুও বলতে পার।” আমি বললাম, “চন্দ্রবিন্দু কেন?” শুনে বেড়ালটা “তাও জানো না?” বলে এক চোখ বুজে ফ্যাচ্‌ফ্যাচ্ করে বিশ্রীরকম হাসতে লাগল। আমি ভারি অপ্রস্তুত হয়ে গেলাম। মনে হল, ঐ চন্দ্রবিন্দুর কথাটা নিশ্চয় আমার বোঝা উচিত ছিল। তাই থতমত খেয়ে তাড়াতাড়ি বলে ফেললাম, “ও হ্যাঁ-হ্যাঁ, বুঝতে পেরেছি।” বেড়ালটা খুশি হয়ে বলল, “হ্যাঁ, এ তো বোঝাই যাচ্ছে— চন্দ্রবিন্দুর চ, বেড়ালের তালব্য শ, রুমালের মা— হল চশমা। কেমন, হল তো?”

Sourendro-Soumyojit: Sourendro Mullick & Soumyojit Das, the Pianist - Vocalist musician duo has won over hearts across the world by their touching renditions of short classical pieces, semi-classical pieces, ghazals, thumri, new compositions, popular songs, playback songs. The duo composes, performs, and interprets music they believe in, they keep the faith of their connoisseurs, and they worship art. Be prepared to be swept away by their music on Saturday, October 1.

Shovan-Trisha: Shovan is the winner of Zee Bangla Sa Re Ga Ma Pa and Trisha is a household name today in India, performing many of the foot-tapping Bengali and Bollywood peppy numbers. They will team up to mesmerize you with an incredible musical night (Sunday, October 2) to remember for years to come.
